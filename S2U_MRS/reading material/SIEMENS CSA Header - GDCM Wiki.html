Meaning

Ref:

  • http://radiology.ucsd.edu/radres/documents/Neuro/NeuroMRProtocols-Siemens.pdf

KEY: The following are definitions of headings and abbreviations used in the protocols above. 

BW: Bandwidth
CONTRAST (CE): Gd (gadolinium, 0.2ml/kg, IV to a maximum of 20 ml).
CSI: Chemical Shift Imaging (multivoxel MR spectroscopy)
DWI: Diffusion weighted imaging
EPI: Echo-planar imaging
ET: Echo train for turbo spin echo sequence.
FC: Flow compensation technique which utilizes additional gradients to refocus protons out of phase as a result of flow.
FISP: Fast Imaging with Steady-state free Precession.
FLASH (FL): Fast Low Angle Shot – gradient-echo sequence with a spoiler gradient after the readout.
FLAIR: Fluid attenuated inversion recovery
FOV: Field of view for the acquisition (along the long axis if a rectangular FOV is used).
FS: Fat saturation
GRE: Gradient recalled echo; also known as FLASH or FISP.
IntLv Interleave or concatenated – used for contiguous or overlapping sections.
MATRIX: Number of data points acquired in the phase-encoding direction. The frequency direction is set at 256 data points, except 512 for T2 TSE in
the brain. If a rectangular FOV used, the matrix size is interpolated to a full matrix size (square FOV).
MPRAGE: Magnetization-prepared rapid acquisition gradient echo sequence.
NEX: Number of excitations. The number of times the acquisition sequence is repeated to increase signal to noise ratio.
OvrS: Percent over sampling in the phase direction to prevent phase wrap-around.
PRESS: Point-Resolved Spectroscopy
PWI: Perfusion weighted imaging
ST: Saturation subroutine which utilizes additional radiofrequency pulses to saturate spins outside the imaging volume immediately prior to each
slice select gradient to reduce artifacts. Pulses can be applied in one or all of 6 planes: S - superior, I - inferior, A - anterior, P - posterior, R -
right, L - left, C/S or C/I - moving sat pulses for time-of-flight MR angio sequences.
PLANE: Anatomic plane of imaging
SLSP : Interslice spacing (mm). InterLv - interleaving results in a 100% slice gap between images with an automatic second acquisition that images
the slice gaps. Results in images with no slice gaps and without crosstalk artifact.
SLTHK: Slice thickness (mm)
TE: Echo time (msec)
2D TOF: MR angio sequence using multislice acquisition and time-of-flight technique.
3D TOF: MR angio sequence using volume acquisition and time-of-flight technique.
TR: Pulse repetition time (msec)
TSE: Turbo spin-echo; same as fast spin echo
3D CISS: Volume acquisition using Constructive Interference in Steady State pulse sequence
